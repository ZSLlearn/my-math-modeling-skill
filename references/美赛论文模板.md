# MCM/ICM Paper Template (美赛论文模板)

> **This template is designed for the Mathematical Contest in Modeling (MCM) and Interdisciplinary Contest in Modeling (ICM).**
> **Language: English only.**
>
> **💡 Flexibility Statement**: This template provides a **suggested chapter structure**, not a rigid prescription. You are encouraged to:
>
> - Merge short adjacent sections where they flow better
> - Add new sections for content that deserves its own space (e.g., "Exploratory Data Analysis," "Comparison of Alternative Approaches," "Practical Recommendations")
> - Re-order sections if a different flow better tells your story
> - The hard constraints below are competition rules — the chapter structure is your creative choice

## ⚠️ MCM/ICM Guidelines

| Item                            | Requirement                                                                                  |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Page Limit**            | 20–30 pages (including everything except title page)                                        |
| **Summary**               | Critical! Must include background → methods → results → significance + innovations        |
| **Language**              | English — natural, fluent, no machine-translation feel                                      |
| **Format**                | LaTeX strongly recommended; Word acceptable                                                  |
| **References**            | APA or MLA (consistent throughout); cite all external sources                                |
| **Plagiarism**            | Zero tolerance — all sources must be properly cited                                         |
| **Innovation**            | The highest-weighted criterion — originality and creativity matter above all                |
| **AI Use Report**         | **Required since 2024** — must disclose AI tools used and how they were used (see Appendix)  |
| **Title Page**            | COMAP provides a Control Sheet; do NOT include team/advisor info in paper body              |
| **ICM Special (C/D/E/F)** | Must include interdisciplinary perspectives (social/ethical/policy/environmental dimensions) |

## Summary

> **⚠️ THE MOST CRITICAL SECTION OF YOUR PAPER. The Summary is read first and often determines your paper's fate. It must stand alone as a complete miniature of your work.**

[Your Summary should be approximately 1 page and include the following elements:]

- **Background & Problem** (1–2 sentences): *What real-world problem does this paper address?*
- **Key Challenge** (1 sentence): *What made this problem particularly difficult?*
- **Our Approach** (2–3 sentences): *What model(s) did we develop? What was the key innovation?*
- **Methodology** (2–3 sentences): *How did we solve it? What techniques did we apply?*
- **Key Results** (3–4 sentences): *What did we find? **Include specific numbers.***
- **Validation** (1–2 sentences): *How did we test the model's reliability?*
- **Broader Implications** (1–2 sentences): *Why does this matter beyond the specific problem?*

**Summary Writing Checklist**:

- [ ] Does the first sentence immediately convey what is novel?
- [ ] Are there at least **3 specific numerical results** in the Summary?
- [ ] Is every sentence carrying substantive information (no filler)?
- [ ] Is the innovation clearly stated?
- [ ] Does it end with a meaningful takeaway?

**MCM/ICM Template Instructions**:

1. This template provides a **suggested structure** — adjust section numbers, titles, merge, split, or add sections as your problem demands. Do not feel constrained by the exact chapter count or naming.
2. **The Summary is make-or-break.** Spend at least 20% of your total paper time refining it.
3. **Add 3–5 Keywords** after the Summary — these help judges quickly grasp your paper's scope.
4. Every assumption must have a justification AND impact assessment. This separates O/F papers from M/H papers.
5. **Some problems require a letter or memo** (e.g., "Write a one-page letter to the editor"). If applicable, add a standalone section before References (see Section 12 of the output example).
6. Use LaTeX for professional typesetting. Convey your ideas clearly and naturally — avoid stilted, machine-translated English.
7. Graphs must be publication-quality. Label axes, use clear legends, choose color schemes that are distinct when printed in grayscale.
8. For ICM problems (C/D/E/F), ensure you address interdisciplinary dimensions — social, ethical, policy, or environmental implications.
9. Be honest about limitations and suggest specific improvements. Judges reward intellectual honesty over overclaiming.
10. **The AI Use Report (Appendix A) is mandatory per COMAP 2024+ rules.** Disclose all AI tools used, how they were used, and that the team reviewed all output.
11. The paper should tell a story: "Here is a problem → Here is what made it hard → Here is how we solved it → Here is what we found → Here is why it matters."
